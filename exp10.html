Aim:

Measure the initial velocity of a steel ball using a Vernier Projectile Launcher. Apply the ideas from two-dimensional kinematics to predict the impact of a ball in projectile motion.

Apparatus:

Vernier Projectile Launcher Small cardboard box, Steel ball, Meter stick, Waxed paper

Theory:

The final momentum of the particle may be obtained by vectorially adding its initial momentum and the impulse during the time of impact.A collision between two bodies which occurs in a very small interval of time and during which the two bodies exert relatively large forces on each other, is called an impact. The common normal to the surfaces in contact during the impact is known as the line of impact. If the velocities of impact and if their mass centres too are located on this line, the impact is said to be a direct central impact. If a ball of mass m collides with a concrete floor with a velocity V, the ball rebounds back with a velocity V . If the ball is dropped on to be floor, V and V lie on a vertical line. Mass centre of the ball lies on this line. The centre of the infinite massed floor also can be assumed to be lying on the same line. Hence the impact is a direct central one, nothing that floor has zero velocity. The collision period consists of two district phases.The restitution phase that follows at the end of which the ball and the floor will have regained their original shape and the ball rebounds back with a velocity V´. Force P and R exerted by the floor on the ball are the only impulsive forces during the impact.
